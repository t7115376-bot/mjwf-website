§ 01 Who we work with

Our partners.

Research bodies, hospitals, medical colleges, and a referral hospital, each one essential to making the work real.

01

10zyme

Health innovation · United Kingdom

A UK health innovation company developing accessible cervical screening technology. Together we conducted Phase 1 of our cervical cancer research across Khagaria and Bhagalpur. Dr Angela Pyne of 10zyme has continued as a key contributor into Phase 2, helping shape the CPD training programme now being delivered.

02

University of Essex

Academic research · United Kingdom

In partnership with the University of Essex, we conducted Phase 2 of our cervical cancer research: a qualitative study with healthcare workers and doctors across Bihpur and Purnia. Together we developed the CPD training programme being delivered in June 2026.

03

Bhagalpur Medical College

Medical college · Bihar

Bhagalpur Medical College granted ethical approval for our Phase 1 cervical cancer research, allowing us to conduct a rigorous, credible study across two districts.

04

Bihpur Primary Health Centre

Government healthcare · Bhagalpur

A government healthcare facility in Bhagalpur district. Their healthcare workers and doctors opened their doors to us for Phase 2, speaking honestly about what they face every day. What they shared with us is at the heart of the training we built.

05

Fatma Hospital, Purnia

Private healthcare · Purnia

A private healthcare facility in Purnia. Their team sat with us alongside Bihpur PHC, giving us a full picture of what both public and private sector healthcare workers are dealing with on the ground.

06

Referral Hospital Gogri

Referral hospital · Khagaria

When someone sits down at our health camp and finds out for the first time that their blood pressure is dangerously high, they need somewhere real to go. Referral Hospital Gogri is that place. We have a working relationship so that every person we refer actually gets seen.

§ 02 Cervical cancer research

A collaboration in three stages.

Our cervical cancer work was built with partners across India and the UK, from ethical approval to fieldwork to a training programme now being delivered.

Phase 1 · with 10zyme

We asked what people actually know.

A quantitative study across Khagaria and Bhagalpur, with ethical approval from Bhagalpur Medical College, measuring how much the community, healthcare workers, and doctors really understand about cervical cancer. The answer was very little.

Phase 2 · with University of Essex

We listened to those delivering care.

A qualitative study with healthcare workers and doctors at Bihpur PHC and Fatma Hospital, Purnia, through group discussions to understand the barriers they face on the ground. What they told us shaped everything that came next.

2026 · CPD training

We built training around the real problem.

From those conversations we designed a CPD training programme that addresses what healthcare workers actually face, not the assumed problem. It is being delivered in June 2026.
